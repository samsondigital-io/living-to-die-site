---
title: "Echoes of Truth"
description: "A poem reflecting on the importance of speaking truth in the face of institutional silence."
pubDate: 2023-11-12
publication: "Seasons Magazine"
featured: true
---

In sterile halls where whispers die,
And charts speak louder than the cry
Of mothers, daughters, sisters, wives—
Who beg to be heard in fights for lives.

The mammogram shows clear, they said,
While cancer spreads with silent tread.
But Brenda knew what doctors missed:
That truth exists when lies persist.

Eight years of battle, court and prayer,
Against a system unaware
That healing starts with listening ears,
Not just with protocols and fears.

Her voice rings out beyond the grave,
For all the women she would save
From negligence wrapped up in white—
Who trust but learn they have to fight.

In every exam room, let it sound:
The echo of her truth profound.
That patients' instincts matter more
Than pride behind a clinic door.

*For Brenda, who taught us that justice and healing begin with being heard.*
